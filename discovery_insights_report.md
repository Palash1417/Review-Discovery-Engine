# Spotify Music Discovery — Findings
Executive summary: This study analyzed 161 Spotify music discovery reviews, revealing a predominantly negative sentiment (59%) towards the service. Users struggle with various aspects of music discovery, including repetition, poor recommendations, and limited control over their listening experience. The most significant frustrations stem from the repetition filter bubble (100% negative) and search and navigation issues (77% negative). By understanding these challenges, Spotify can improve its music discovery features to better meet user needs.

## Why do users struggle to discover new music?
Users struggle to discover new music due to various issues, including poor recommendations, repetition, and limited control over their listening experience. The **repetition_filter_bubble** cluster (n=11) highlights the frustration with the repetition of songs in Spotify's shuffle feature, with one user stating, "the shuffle literally only plays the same 20 songs on repeat in the same order." Additionally, the **discovery_features** cluster (n=17) reveals that users are frustrated with ads, "smart" shuffle, and the inability to select specific songs to play without a premium account. These issues hinder users' ability to discover new music, leading to a negative sentiment towards the service.

## What are the most common frustrations with recommendations?
The most common frustrations with recommendations are related to the repetition of songs, poor personalization, and excessive ads. The **repetition_filter_bubble** cluster (n=11) shows that 100% of users in this cluster are negative about the repetition issue, with one user stating, "smart shuffle option does not change that" (i.e., hearing the same song 10 times in a short period). The **algorithm_personalization** cluster (n=8) also reveals frustrations with broken or inaccurate music recommendation algorithms, with one user saying, "every playlist is full of [AI slop]." These frustrations indicate that users are not satisfied with the current recommendation system.

## What listening behaviors are users trying to achieve?
Users are trying to achieve uninterrupted listening experiences, control over their playlists, and discovery of new music. The **other** cluster (n=48) highlights users' goals, such as listening to music without interruptions and having control over playback features. One user states, "I want to listen to music without interruptions, have control over playback, and enjoy a seamless music experience." The **playlist_curation** cluster (n=15) also reveals that users want to discover new music, create and listen to personalized playlists, and have control over their listening experience. These goals are consistently mentioned across various clusters, emphasizing the importance of user control and seamless listening experiences.

## What causes users to repeatedly listen to the same content?
Users repeatedly listen to the same content due to the repetition filter bubble issue, which is caused by the algorithm's tendency to play the same songs over and over. The **repetition_filter_bubble** cluster (n=11) shows that 100% of users in this cluster are negative about this issue, with one user stating, "the shuffle literally only plays the same 20 songs on repeat in the same order." This issue leads to a lack of discovery of new music, causing users to listen to the same content repeatedly.

## Which user segments experience different discovery challenges?
The user segments that experience different discovery challenges are those who are frustrated with search and navigation (77% negative), repetition filter bubble (100% negative), and algorithm personalization (62% negative). The **search_and_navigation** cluster (n=30) reveals that users are frustrated with the new widget, ads, and forced features that slow down the app, making it difficult to navigate and listen to music in the desired order. In contrast, users who are satisfied with the service (34% positive) tend to appreciate the discovery features, such as Discover Weekly, with one user stating, "Discover Weekly reads my mind." These differences in sentiment and challenges highlight the need for personalized solutions to address the diverse needs of Spotify users.

## What unmet needs emerge consistently across reviews?
The unmet needs that emerge consistently across reviews are the desire for an ad-free or minimal-ad experience, more control over playback features, and a more stable and responsive app. The **other** cluster (n=48) highlights these unmet needs, with one user stating, "I wish for an ad-free or minimal-ad experience, more control over playback features, and a more stable and responsive app." The **discovery_features** cluster (n=17) also reveals that users wish for more control over playlist shuffling, fewer ads, and more accurate music recommendations without needing a premium account. These unmet needs are consistently mentioned across various clusters, emphasizing the importance of addressing these issues to improve user satisfaction.

## Limitations
This study has several limitations. The dataset is single-source, consisting of reviews from the Google Play store, which may not be representative of all Spotify users. The data is also recent and English-language, which may introduce biases in the results. Additionally, the study only analyzed 161 reviews, which may not be sufficient to capture the full range of user experiences and opinions. Future studies should aim to collect data from multiple sources, including different app stores, languages, and user segments, to provide a more comprehensive understanding of Spotify music discovery challenges and opportunities.

## Appendix B — Per-Cluster Evidence

### Cluster: other (n=48)
Here is a summary of the cluster in 4 short labeled parts:

**FRUSTRATIONS**: Users are frustrated with excessive ads, frequent app crashes, slow and buggy performance, and limited features in the free version, such as forced shuffling and inability to rewind playback.

**GOALS**: Users want to listen to music without interruptions, have control over playback, and enjoy a seamless music experience.

**UNMET NEEDS**: Users wish for an ad-free or minimal-ad experience, more control over playback features, and a more stable and responsive app.

**QUOTES**: 
1. "Full ads after 1 song... highly not recommended"
2. "Spotify's free experience has become very frustrating. There are too many unskippable ads, sometimes 3–5 in a row, and basic features like song selection, repeat, and proper shuffle are locked behind Premium."

### Cluster: search_and_navigation (n=30)
Here is a summary of the cluster in 4 short labeled parts:

**FRUSTRATIONS**: Users are frustrated with the new widget, ads, and forced features that slow down the app, making it difficult to navigate and listen to music in the desired order.

**GOALS**: Users want to easily find and listen to specific songs or playlists in the order they choose, without interruptions or forced features.

**UNMET NEEDS**: Users wish for optional features, such as the ability to delete podcast lists, choose their own widget layout, and have more control over their listening experience.

**QUOTES**: 
1. "HATE THE NEW WIDGET!!! Bring back the regular now playing widget, at least give us the option!!!"
2. "I dont want ai playlists or mixes I want to stream music quickly and latley Spotify hasn't been doing so much in terms of that"
